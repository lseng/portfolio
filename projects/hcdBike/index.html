Bicurity Security App

A personal project seeking to alleviate bike theft on the UC Berkeley campus.

intro

Background

We started out with the thought of whether or not there exists a problem with the way our students bike around campus or not. We set out to do observations on their behavioral process. We studied how they spent their days and came up with a list of their most common problems as bikers.
Defining the Problem

Bikes are not secure, they keep getting stolen. Students are frustrated about security. Students frequently report stolen bikes, or parts of them, around campus weekly. This problem has increased dramatically lately as more and more students come to study at Cal.
The Goal

Create a system in which students can keep track of bike safety, and have a peace of mind when not around their bikes.

01

Insights From Preliminary Research

Safety: This entails both protecting bikes from being stolen or vandalized and feeling safe from hostile traffic and pedestrians.
Predictability: Bikers on the Berkeley campus struggle with the unpredictable nature of finding a space to park their bike-- this often hinders their ability to schedule their time.
Speed and Convenience: The main reason why students invest in biking as their main mode of transport to classes is because it offers speed and convenience. But, given the crowded, mercurial conditions of the campus and difficulties situating the bikes before heading off to class or the store, it becomes tougher for the students to pursue these values.

How Might We

How might we efficiently create a product to increase security, and provide ease of access? Consequently, this results in an application idea, where hardware and software work together to address these pain points.
Low-Fidelity Prototyping

Low-Fidelity Prototypes

Here is the initial prototyping ideation phase. I've gone through a bunch of hardware creations to aid with the needs. However, I realized that these designs pose even greater security threat as the bikes are now seem more advanced and expensive, complicating the issue of theft. Furthermore, it is also more expensive on the user's behalf.
How Might We: Restructuring

Counting the costs and benefits of each idea in my low-fidelity prototypes, I've decided to change the "How Might We" question. How might we create a cost effective, yet discrete method of tracking bike security and ease of usage? This fits well withint the target demographic I am aiming for: college students. One small hardware piece and an application that communicates through the cloud makes a good product that balances between cost and function.

Theft Research

Thousands of bikes have been stolen in the past. Though the numbers have been decreasing, it is still a major problem compared to other issues on any college campus.Bike theft is going down, but is still prevalent. 4.31 per every 1,000 people have bikes stolen on average per all college campuses. Since Berkeley is a crowded campus, theft rate is likely higher.

Our app works by allowing you to track where your bike is, how many spaces are there for your bike parking, and the ability to submit direct reports to the local police in case of theft, via our Local Law Enforcement Contract.
